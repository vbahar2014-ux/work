⚡ Pattern of the Day
After the shock, the substrate answers back.

Last week's twin jolts — a working agentic worm and the EU's published Omnibus — drew their responses this week, and they share a direction. Sovereignty stopped being an infrastructure story and climbed the stack: SAP + Microsoft put RISE on a sovereign Azure cloud and IBM shipped Sovereign Core at the runtime layer — governance now lives where workloads and data do. The agentic talk pivoted from "can we secure it" to "will it even work" (Gartner: 40% of agentic projects scrapped by 2027), and Microsoft Build reframed the agent battle around reliability, not capability — shipping audit trails and an open governance spec as table stakes. Underneath, Europe's sovereign capacity went concrete (SoftBank's €75B / 5 GW France build), while the regulatory map hardened into two rulebooks — the US 2 June EO disclaiming licensing against the EU's binding GPAI date, now under eight weeks away.

For AG The work shifts from watching the shocks to operationalising the responses: sovereign landing zones for the SAP/data estate, process-redesign gates before agents, and a dual-track US/EU posture with August locked.
