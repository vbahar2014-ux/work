⚡ Pattern of the Day
Sovereignty stops being a product and becomes a rule.

Yesterday sovereignty climbed the stack into things you could buy — SAP's sovereign Azure cloud, IBM's Sovereign Core. Today the EU turned it into law: the Cloud and AI Development Act grades cloud and AI sovereignty into four assurance levels — Level 3 demanding EU ownership and staff citizenship — while Gartner attached a number to the migration: >75% of EMEA enterprises geopatriating by 2030, up from under 5% today. The same hardening hit agents: the EU AI Office moved from advice to obligation, now requiring deterministic audit logs for high-risk agentic systems, as open standards (agnt8x's Agent Manifest/Passport) raced to supply the primitives. Underneath, the data-center power constraint monetised — Applied Digital's $5.2B / 210 MW lease and Apollo's $35B / 20 GW Broadcom vehicle show secured power is now the investment-grade asset, with PJM ~6 GW short for 2027 and capacity prices up 833%.

For AG The sovereign-first posture is now a compliance asset, not just a hedge. Map the SAP/data estate to a target CADA assurance level, make deterministic agent audit logs a hard build gate, and treat pre-secured power as a first-class sourcing criterion.
